# Customer Segments

## Concept

Not all LocalStack customers look alike, and understanding the segment tiers helps a TAM calibrate expectations, communication style, and prioritization.

**The three broad segments:**
- **Individual developers (Hobby / free tier)** — often self-serve, non-commercial use, minimal or no direct TAM touch. This segment is largely product-led: they self-onboard, self-support via docs/community, and rarely interact with a human at LocalStack.
- **Small teams (self-serve, Base tier)** — small companies or teams within larger orgs adopting LocalStack informally, often started by an individual engineer who then expanded usage. Light-touch account management; may occasionally need TAM help if usage grows or a technical blocker appears, but not a dedicated relationship.
- **Enterprise (Ultimate tier, dedicated support)** — larger organizations with formal contracts, custom deployment considerations, and typically a dedicated TAM relationship. These accounts justify the QBR/EBR cadence, health scoring, and proactive relationship management described elsewhere in this series.

**Why the segmentation matters:**
- It determines **touch cadence** — you don't run a QBR for a $0 Hobby account, but you likely do for an Enterprise account
- It determines **how issues get prioritized** — an Enterprise account blocked on a parity gap carries more urgency than the same gap affecting a Hobby user
- It reflects the **PLG (product-led growth) + top-down enterprise sales** dual motion common in dev tools: many Enterprise deals actually originate from a bottom-up adoption story (an individual developer's usage grows into a team, then an enterprise contract) rather than a traditional top-down sales process

**Why this matters for a TAM:** you'll spend the vast majority of your direct relationship time on the Enterprise segment, but understanding the full funnel — including how self-serve usage can organically become an enterprise opportunity — is core to spotting expansion signals early.
