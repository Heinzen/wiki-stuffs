# Adjacent Products

## Concept

LocalStack is expanding beyond pure AWS emulation, and a TAM needs to understand this broader portfolio to correctly frame growth and expansion conversations — not just support the core AWS product.

**Key product to know: LocalStack for Snowflake**
- A separate emulator focused on **local Snowflake development and testing**, distinct from the core AWS-focused product.
- It recently moved to its **own standalone Docker image** for faster startup, decoupled from the main LocalStack image.
- It includes ongoing **SQL parity improvements**, mirroring the same "parity" concept from the AWS product but applied to Snowflake's SQL dialect and behavior.
- The underlying value proposition is the same as the core product: local, fast, cost-free testing/development against a service that's normally remote and billed by usage — just applied to a data warehouse instead of cloud infrastructure.

**Why this matters strategically:**
- It signals LocalStack's ambition to be a broader **"local emulation of expensive cloud services"** platform, not a single-purpose AWS tool.
- For a TAM, this creates a natural **cross-sell/expansion conversation**: a customer already using LocalStack for AWS testing who also works with Snowflake is a plausible expansion target.
- It also means account management is starting to become **multi-product** for some customers — coordinating renewal/health conversations across more than one product surface, similar to how many SaaS companies eventually manage a portfolio rather than a single product.

**Why this matters for a TAM:** being able to speak credibly about where the company is expanding — not just the product you support today — signals strategic awareness in interviews and, on the job, is exactly the kind of insight that helps you spot expansion opportunities before a customer raises them.
