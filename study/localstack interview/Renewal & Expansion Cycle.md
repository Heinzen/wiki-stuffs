# Renewal & Expansion Cycle

## Concept

The renewal/expansion cycle is the recurring rhythm of proving and growing account value over the life of a contract — and it's arguably the central business process a TAM operates within.

**Key components:**
- **Renewal** — keeping a contract at its current value; the baseline outcome a TAM protects
- **Expansion** — growing the contract (higher tier, more seats, more usage-based consumption); the upside outcome a TAM helps identify and support
- **QBR cadence** — for a dev-tools company like LocalStack, a QBR typically reviews usage stats (services emulated, CI run volume), test coverage growth, and alignment with the parity/feature roadmap — distinct from a typical SaaS QBR that might focus more on seat utilization or feature adoption within a UI
- **Health signals feeding the cycle** — usage trend, support ticket patterns, champion engagement, and parity gaps all roll up into whether a renewal looks safe or at-risk, and whether an expansion conversation is timely
- **Timing** — renewal conversations typically start well before the actual contract end date (often 90+ days out for Enterprise accounts), giving time to address risk factors or build an expansion case before the decision point

**Why the LocalStack-specific version differs from generic SaaS:**
- Value is proven through **technical outcomes** (time/cost saved vs. real AWS sandboxes, defects caught pre-production) rather than softer engagement metrics like login frequency
- Expansion is often **usage-driven and technical** — a customer needing a higher tier because they've grown into services or CI volume their current tier doesn't cover, rather than a seat-based growth story
- Parity roadmap alignment is a recurring QBR topic in a way that's unique to a product built around emulating an external, evolving system (AWS)

**Why this matters for a TAM:** the renewal/expansion cycle is the business outcome your entire day-to-day technical work ultimately serves — every parity conversation, escalation, and QBR is, in part, an input into whether this cycle ends in retention, growth, or churn.
