# Sales Motion

## Concept

Understanding how a company acquires and grows customers shapes how a TAM should think about their role in the broader revenue lifecycle — TAMs don't close deals, but their work directly feeds the sales motion.

**The dual motion at a company like LocalStack:**
- **Product-Led Growth (PLG)** — individual developers discover and adopt LocalStack on their own, often through documentation, GitHub, or word of mouth, with no salesperson involved. Usage grows organically within a team before any commercial conversation happens.
- **Top-down enterprise sales** — a traditional sales motion where an Account Executive engages a company's leadership or platform team directly, often for larger, more strategic deals where the customer wants dedicated support, custom terms, or security/compliance guarantees from day one.

**Where TAMs sit relative to this:**
- TAMs typically engage **after** the initial sale — whether that sale originated bottom-up (PLG) or top-down (enterprise sales) — and are responsible for account health, adoption, and retention from that point forward.
- In PLG-originated accounts, a TAM may be the **first real human relationship** the customer has with the company, which shapes trust significantly.
- In top-down enterprise accounts, a TAM often inherits an account with pre-set expectations from the sales process, and needs to quickly validate technical fit against what was promised commercially.
- **Expansion** frequently bridges both motions: a PLG account that grows in usage becomes a candidate for a formal enterprise conversation, and a TAM's usage insight is often the trigger for that motion-shift conversation with an Account Manager or sales counterpart.

**Why this matters for a TAM:** knowing whether an account originated PLG or top-down changes how you approach the relationship — a PLG account needs relationship-building from scratch, while a top-down account needs you to validate and deliver on pre-sale promises.
